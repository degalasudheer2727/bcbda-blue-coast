At Bhattiprolu stands a stupa dating to roughly the 3rd century BCE. Excavations in 1892 yielded inscribed stone relic caskets holding crystal, silver and gold caskets with relics and jewels. Its inscriptions preserve an early Brahmi variant — the ‘Bhattiprolu script’ — considered a likely precursor of the modern Telugu script, a landmark of South Indian epigraphy.
