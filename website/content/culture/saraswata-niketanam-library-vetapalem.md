Founded in 1918, Saraswata Niketanam in Vetapalem is one of India's oldest private non-profit libraries, holding around 90,000 volumes plus palm-leaf and unpublished manuscripts in Telugu, Sanskrit, Hindi, Urdu and English. Its foundation stone was laid by Mahatma Gandhi in 1929, and President Rajendra Prasad visited in 1935 — a deep tie to the freedom movement.
