# Governance Framework
### Bapatla–Chirala Beach Development Authority (BCBDA)

*Draft v0.1 — June 2026*

---

## 1. Why a dedicated authority

Coastal development in India routinely fails at the seams *between* departments — Tourism builds, Fisheries objects, Irrigation owns the foreshore, Pollution Control regulates, the Panchayat governs the village, and no one is accountable for the coast as a whole. BCBDA is proposed as a **single, empowered, accountable body** with a clear remit over one corridor, a statutory master plan, and its own balance sheet.

## 2. Legal form

Recommended: a **statutory Special Area Development Authority** constituted by the Government of Andhra Pradesh (e.g., under the relevant state Metropolitan/Urban/Area Development Authority or Tourism legislation), with a notified development area and powers to plan, permit, develop, levy, and enforce within it.

Key legal attributes:
- A **notified BCBDA area** (the corridor + hinterland defined in the Master Plan).
- A **statutory master plan** that binds all development in the area.
- Power to act as the **single-window** sanctioning authority (within CRZ and state law).
- Power to **own, lease, concession, and operate** assets; to **levy fees/charges**; and to **raise finance**.
- A ring-fenced **BCBDA Fund** into which all corridor revenues flow.

### Governing Board
Sets policy, approves the master plan, budget, and major concessions. Composition balances **government** (Tourism, Fisheries, Forests/Environment, Irrigation, R&B, Pollution Control, Disaster Management, district administration), **independent experts** (coastal ecology, urban planning, finance, tourism), and **community representatives** (fisherfolk cooperatives, women's SHGs, local bodies).

### Executive (CEO + functional wings)
Delivers the plan day-to-day across five wings: Planning & Projects; Environment & Resilience; Finance & Revenue; Operations & Maintenance; Community & Livelihoods.

### Advisory & participatory bodies
- **Advisory Council / Expert Panel** — technical guidance and independent review.
- **Community Assembly** — a standing forum giving fishing and farming communities a formal, recurring voice and a role in benefit-sharing decisions.

## 4. Powers & instruments

| Instrument | Use |
|---|---|
| Statutory Master Plan | Binds all development; basis for permissions |
| Single-window sanction | Faster, coordinated, CRZ-aware approvals |
| Concessions & leases (PPP) | Private capital & operations under public standards |
| Fees, rents, user charges | Recurring own-revenue |
| BCBDA Fund | Ring-fenced corridor finance |
| Development charges / betterment levy | Capture uplift in land value to fund public works |
| Bonds / blended finance | Capital for major works |
| Environmental & building standards | Enforce Blue Flag / CRZ / green-building norms |

## 5. Accountability & transparency

Transparency is a **design principle, not an afterthought**:

- **Open by default:** master plan, budgets, board minutes, concession terms, environmental and footfall data, and project status are published (this public repository and the website are the first instruments).
- **Independent audit:** annual financial audit + an annual **environmental & social audit** against the KPIs in [`data/kpis.json`](../data/kpis.json).
- **Citizen feedback & grievance redress:** public reporting channels, response SLAs, and a published register.
- **Performance reporting:** a live public dashboard tracks the Charter's KPIs.

## 6. Inter-agency coordination

BCBDA does not replace line departments — it **coordinates** them within the corridor via the Board, MoUs, and the single-window process, with clear escalation to the state government for disputes. The Authority is the *integrator*; departments remain the *specialists*.

## 7. Community ownership model

A distinguishing feature: communities are **stakeholders, not bystanders**.
- Reserved Board and Community Assembly seats.
- **Cooperative & SHG concessions** for shacks, F&B, watersports, homestays, and clean-up services.
- A **Community Benefit Share** from corridor revenues funding local services and a livelihood fund.
- First right to jobs and skilling (see [05](05-financing-and-economics.md) and [04](04-sustainability-ecosystem.md)).
