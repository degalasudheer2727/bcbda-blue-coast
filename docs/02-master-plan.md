# Coastal Master Plan
### Bapatla–Chirala Beach Development Authority (BCBDA)

*Draft v0.1 — June 2026 · Spatial & development framework for the Suryalanka–Vodarevu–Ramapuram corridor*

---

## 1. Purpose & scope

This Master Plan translates the [Vision & Charter](01-vision-and-charter.md) into a spatial and development framework for the BCBDA notified area: the Bay of Bengal shoreline of Bapatla District stretching from **Suryalanka (Bapatla Beach)** in the north, through **Vodarevu** and **Ramapuram** near Chirala in the south, together with the immediate hinterland, access corridors, estuaries, and fishing settlements that depend on the coast.

It is a **strategic master plan** — it sets the structure, zoning logic, project pipeline, and standards. Detailed engineering, environmental impact assessment (EIA), and CRZ clearances follow at project stage.

---

## 2. Planning principles

1. **The beach is the asset.** Everything is organised to protect, not crowd, the active shoreline.
2. **Set back, don't build forward.** Permanent structures stay landward of the dune line and the CRZ High Tide Line buffer; the active beach stays open and public.
3. **Nodes, not sprawl.** Concentrate built activity in a few well-served nodes; keep long stretches of coast as natural / low-impact green-blue corridor.
4. **Move people, not just cars.** A continuous non-motorised spine (walk/cycle/e-shuttle) links the nodes; private vehicles park at the edge.
5. **Layered resilience.** Soft defences (dunes, vegetation, reefs) first; hard defences only where unavoidable; all critical assets above the projected 2050 surge line.
6. **Circular by design.** Energy, water, and waste loops are closed at the corridor scale (see [04](04-sustainability-ecosystem.md)).

## 3. Spatial structure — the "string of pearls"

The corridor is organised as a **green-blue spine** connecting four primary nodes and a series of natural reserves:

```
  N  ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
     │  NODE A — SURYALANKA GATEWAY                   │  Arrival, civic, Blue-Flag beach
     │      │                                         │
     │   [Casuarina & Dune Reserve]                   │  Living shoreline, low impact
     │      │                                         │
     │  NODE B — ECO-ADVENTURE & WELLNESS             │  Eco-resorts, wellness, camping
     │      │                                         │
     │   [Estuary & Mangrove Reserve]                 │  Restoration, birding, nursery
     │      │                                         │
     │  NODE C — VODAREVU MARINE & WATERSPORTS        │  Watersports, fishing harbour link
     │      │                                         │
     │  NODE D — RAMAPURAM QUIET COAST                 │  Low-density, conservation, retreat
  S  └──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
            ── Green-Blue Spine (NMT + e-shuttle) ──
```

### Node A — Suryalanka Gateway (Arrival & Civic)
The corridor's "front door," ~9 km from Bapatla town. Functions: visitor arrival and orientation centre, edge parking & mobility hub, the flagship **Blue Flag beach**, promenade, lifeguard and safety HQ, food courts run by local SHGs, civic plaza, and BCBDA's own offices. Highest footfall, highest standards.

### Node B — Eco-Adventure & Wellness
Low-density eco-resorts, wellness/Ayurveda retreats, glamping and managed camping, nature interpretation, and dune boardwalks set behind the casuarina belt. Premium, low-volume, high-value tourism that funds conservation.

### Node C — Vodarevu Marine & Watersports
Built on Vodarevu's existing draw (~8 km from Chirala) for watersports and fishing. Functions: regulated watersports zone, marine recreation, links to the **Vodarevu fishing harbour** and fisherfolk cooperatives, seafood market and culinary district, boat tours (including dolphin-watching with wildlife-safe protocols).

### Node D — Ramapuram Quiet Coast
The calmest, cleanest stretch — deliberately kept low-density. Conservation-led: protected nesting/roosting areas, a research and education station, and small-scale, high-sensitivity retreats. The "do less, protect more" counterpoint to Node A.

### Natural reserves (the spaces *between* the pearls)
- **Casuarina & Dune Reserve** — restored frontal dunes and casuarina shelterbelts; the corridor's primary soft sea defence.
- **Estuary & Mangrove Reserve** — mangrove restoration on intertidal/high-tidal mudflats, fish nursery, birding, and blue-carbon sink.

## 4. Land-use & zoning framework

A simple, enforceable zoning hierarchy keyed to distance from the High Tide Line (HTL) and CRZ categories:

| Zone | Location | Permitted | Prohibited |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Z0 – Active Beach & Intertidal** | Seaward of dune line / within CRZ no-development buffer | Lifeguarding, temporary removable shacks, clean-up, recreation | Any permanent construction, vehicles, walls |
| **Z1 – Dune & Vegetation Belt** | Frontal dune + casuarina/mangrove | Restoration, boardwalks, interpretation, soft defences | Hard construction, sand mining, clearing |
| **Z2 – Recreation & Amenity** | Behind Z1, within nodes | Promenades, pavilions, showers, lightweight tourism amenities (removable/elevated) | Heavy permanent structures, residential |
| **Z3 – Tourism & Mixed-Use** | Node cores, landward of CRZ limits | Eco-resorts, F&B, retail, civic, parking hubs (elevated, low-rise) | High-rise, polluting industry |
| **Z4 – Community & Livelihood** | Existing settlements/harbour | Housing upgrade, fish processing, markets, cooperatives, services | Displacement, incompatible industry |
| **Z5 – Conservation & Blue-Carbon** | Estuary, mangrove, reserves | Restoration, research, low-impact eco-tourism | Construction, aquaculture conversion |

**Height & density rule of thumb:** low-rise (generally ≤ G+2 near the coast), stepping down toward the sea; elevated/stilted construction in flood-prone areas; generous open space ratios; site coverage minimised to protect drainage and recharge.

## 5. Mobility & access

- **Green-Blue Spine:** a continuous shaded non-motorised corridor (walking + cycling) the length of the coast, paired with a low-emission **e-shuttle** loop linking all four nodes.
- **Park at the edge:** private vehicles terminate at node-edge mobility hubs; the shoreline is vehicle-free.
- **Regional links:** upgraded last-mile road connections to NH/State highways and the Bapatla & Chirala rail stations; EV charging at every hub.
- **Marine mobility:** regulated jetties for tour and fishing boats at Node C; emphasis on electric/clean propulsion over time.
- **Universal access:** beach wheelchairs, accessible matting, ramps, and tactile wayfinding at Node A as a Blue Flag requirement.

---

## 6. Blue & green infrastructure

- **Living shoreline first:** dune nourishment and fencing, casuarina shelterbelts, mangrove restoration at the estuary, and where needed submerged reef/oyster structures to dissipate wave energy — providing coastal defence *and* fish habitat.
- **Hard defences only as last resort,** designed to work *with* soft systems and never to starve downdrift beaches of sand.
- **Stormwater as a resource:** bioswales, recharge wells, and retention wetlands that clean runoff before it reaches the sea (a Blue Flag water-quality essential).
- **Dark-sky & turtle-safe lighting** along the entire frontage to protect nesting wildlife and night ambiance.

## 8. Phasing

- **Phase 1 — Foundations (Years 1–3):** Establish the Authority; notify the master plan; deliver the Suryalanka Blue Flag beach, micro-grid, waste system, dune restoration, and gateway. *Goal: a visible, certifiable "proof beach."*
- **Phase 2 — Build-out (Years 4–6):** Extend to Vodarevu and the estuary; launch watersports, culinary district, eco-resort concessions, the green-blue spine, and the cooperative campus. *Goal: year-round economy and self-funding O&M.*
- **Phase 3 — Maturity & resilience (Years 7–10):** Zero-discharge water system, Ramapuram conservation node, full resilience/early-warning, and the open data digital twin. *Goal: a replicable, self-sustaining model.*

A detailed sequence is in [`06-implementation-roadmap.md`](06-implementation-roadmap.md).

## 9. Design & sustainability standards

- **Blue Flag** environmental, water-quality, safety, services, and education criteria as the baseline for Node A (and template for others).
- **CRZ 2019 compliance** for every structure; maintain HTL buffer; use the Blue Flag facilitation provisions appropriately.
- **Climate-resilient construction:** elevated/stilted where needed; cyclone-rated; critical assets above projected 2050 surge.
- **Green building:** passive cooling, local materials, solar-ready roofs, water-efficient fixtures; net-zero-energy targets for BCBDA's own buildings.
- **Local materiality:** vernacular, low-carbon, repairable; avoid sand mining; source responsibly.

## 10. Risks & safeguards

| Risk | Safeguard |
|---|---|
| Coastal erosion & sea-level rise | Living shorelines, setbacks, monitoring, adaptive design |
| Cyclone / storm surge | Resilient construction, early-warning, shelters, insurance |
| Over-tourism degrading the asset | Node concentration, carrying-capacity limits, conservation reserves |
| Community displacement / inequity | Z4 protections, co-ownership, in-situ upgrade, livelihood-first |
| Pollution / untreated discharge | Zero-discharge water system, waste loops, continuous water-quality testing |
| Financial dependence on grants | Diversified own-revenue model (see [05](05-financing-and-economics.md)) |
| Fragmented governance | Single-window authority with coordinating powers (see [03](03-governance-framework.md)) |
