# Infrastructure & Zones (The Blueprint)
### Bapatla–Chirala Beach Development Authority (BCBDA)

*Draft v0.1 — June 2026 · The physical programme to be built on the ground*

---

## 1. Zoning framework

A simple, enforceable hierarchy keyed to distance from the High Tide Line (HTL):

| Zone | Use | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Z0 – Active Beach & Intertidal** | Lifeguarding, removable shacks, recreation, daily cleaning | No permanent build; vehicle-free |
| **Z1 – Dune & Vegetation Belt** | Dune/casuarina/mangrove restoration, boardwalks | Soft defence only |
| **Z2 – Recreation & Amenity** | Beachfront parks, promenades, pavilions, showers | Lightweight, elevated, removable |
| **Z3 – Tourism & Mixed-Use** | Eco-resorts, F&B, retail, parking hubs, EV charging | Low-rise, landward of CRZ limits |
| **Z4 – Community & Livelihood** | Housing upgrade, fish processing, markets, cooperatives | No displacement; in-situ upgrade |
| **Z5 – Conservation & Blue-Carbon** | Estuary, mangrove, research, low-impact eco-tourism | Protected; no construction |

## 2. Infrastructure programme

### 2.1 Smart parking & mobility hubs
Edge parking at every node (1,000+ ECS combined), multi-level where land is tight, with shuttle pickup so the beach stays vehicle-free. App-based and affordably priced, with **free two-wheeler bays**. Clear signage, EV-ready bays, and accessible parking near every entry.

### 2.2 EV charging network (PPP)
Fast and standard EV chargers at every parking hub and resort, **built and operated by private operators on revenue-share** — zero-capex for the Authority and future-ready for the coastal-highway corridor. Open-standard connectors; app-based payment; green-powered from the corridor micro-grid where possible.

### 2.3 Beachfront parks & green promenades
Family parks, shaded lawns, children's play areas, fitness zones, and a continuous **walking/cycling promenade** behind the dune line — **free public space** at the heart of each node. Native, salt-tolerant landscaping; dark-sky, turtle-safe lighting.

### 2.4 Water games & kids' zones
Safe, shallow water-play and splash zones, seasonal inflatable aqua-parks, and sandcastle/beach-games areas — **affordable family fun**, supervised by lifeguards, sited away from strong-current stretches.

### 2.5 Water sports across the coast
Regulated zones for jet-ski, kayaking, paddle-boarding, parasailing, banana-boat, and surfing lessons — concentrated at **Vodarevu (Node C)**, run by **trained local cooperatives** with safety certification, life-jackets, demarcated channels, and rescue cover.

### 2.6 Helipads & emergency air access
Two corridor helipads for tourism/VIP charter and — critically — **medical and disaster evacuation**, integrated with the early-warning and lifeguard network. Located outside Z0/Z1, with clear approach corridors.

### 2.7 Waterfront markets
Vibrant, organised markets for **seafood, crafts, and street food** — **fixed, affordable stall rents** for local vendors and SHGs, turning footfall into community income. Hygiene standards, cold chain for seafood, and waste-segregation built in.

### 2.8 Beach shacks
Licensed, removable, **design-controlled** beach shacks (Goa-style) operated by local cooperatives, with **capped, transparent pricing** so food and drink stay affordable. Seasonal removal respects Z0 rules and storm season.

### 2.9 Waste management & circular economy
Source-segregation, **composting**, and **material-recovery** centres; **single-use-plastic-free** beaches; refill stations and deposit-return for vendors; **zero-landfill** target. Waste becomes compost and recyclate revenue, not a cost.

### 2.10 Beach-cleaning operations
Daily mechanised + manual cleaning by local **"Blue Brigade"** cooperatives — sand-sifting machines, tide-line patrols, bin networks, and **live cleanliness dashboards**. This is the Blue Flag essential and the single most visible promise to visitors.

## 3. Affordability rules (non-negotiable)

- Capped, published pricing for parking, shacks, markets, and water games.
- Free public beach access and beachfront parks at every node.
- Local cooperatives/SHGs as primary operators, keeping margins and prices fair.
- A reserved share of low-cost stalls and amenities for local vendors.

## 4. Standards & compliance

- **Blue Flag** environmental, safety, services, water-quality, and accessibility criteria.
- **CRZ 2019** for every structure; HTL buffer maintained.
- Cyclone-rated, elevated construction; universal access; turtle/bird-safe lighting.
