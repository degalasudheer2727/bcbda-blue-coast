# Implementation Roadmap
### Bapatla–Chirala Beach Development Authority (BCBDA)

*Draft v0.1 — June 2026 · A 10-year phased delivery sequence*

---

## Overview

Three phases turn the vision into a self-sustaining reality. Each phase has a single headline goal, so progress is unambiguous.

| Phase | Years | Headline goal |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — Foundations** | 1–3 | A visible, certifiable "proof beach" + the Authority established |
| **2 — Build-out** | 4–6 | A year-round economy that covers its own O&M |
| **3 — Maturity & Resilience** | 7–10 | A replicable, fully self-sustaining model |

---

## Phase 0 — Mobilisation (Months 0–6)

*Before the clock starts.*

- Secure Government of AP in-principle approval for the Authority.
- Constitute BCBDA, appoint interim CEO and Board, open the BCBDA Fund.
- Commission baseline studies: coastal survey, ecology, hydrology, footfall, EIA scoping.
- Begin community engagement via the Community Assembly.
- Publish this planning corpus and website; open the GitHub repository.

## Phase 1 — Foundations (Years 1–3)

**Goal: deliver one certifiable Blue Flag beach and prove the model.**

- **P1** Suryalanka Blue Flag Beach & Promenade — clean water, lifeguards, amenities, universal access.
- **P2** Corridor Solar–Wind Micro-grid (initial capacity at Node A).
- **P3** Dune & Casuarina Living-Shoreline Restoration (Suryalanka–Node B).
- **P4** Integrated Waste-to-Resource system + plastic-free beach declaration.
- **P5** Visitor Gateway & Mobility Hub at Node A (park-at-the-edge).
- Notify the statutory Master Plan; stand up single-window approvals.
- Launch first community cooperatives (clean-up, shacks, homestays).
- **Exit criteria:** Suryalanka meets Blue Flag standards; first revenue flowing; community benefit share distributed.

## Phase 2 — Build-out (Years 4–6)

**Goal: a year-round economy that covers O&M from own revenue.**

- **P6** Estuary Mangrove & Blue-Carbon Reserve.
- **P7** Vodarevu Watersports & Seafood Culinary District.
- **P8** Community Cooperative & Skilling Campus.
- **P9** Eco-Resort & Wellness Concessions (PPP) at Node B.
- **P10** Green-Blue Spine — non-motorised corridor + e-shuttle.
- Extend micro-grid, water reuse, and waste loops corridor-wide.
- **Exit criteria:** O&M fully self-funded; year-round occupancy; ≥ 2 cooperatives profitable; second beach on Blue Flag pathway.

## Phase 3 — Maturity & Resilience (Years 7–10)

**Goal: a replicable, self-sustaining, climate-resilient corridor.**

- **P11** Tertiary Wastewater Reuse & Zero-Discharge System (corridor-wide).
- **P12** Ramapuram Conservation & Research Station.
- **P13** Coastal Resilience & Early-Warning System.
- **P14** Digital Twin & Open Coastal Data Platform.
- Pursue second Blue Flag certification; publish the replication playbook for AP's wider coast.
- **Exit criteria:** all Charter KPIs on track; ≥ 90% renewable; zero untreated discharge; ≥ 60% capex self/blended-financed.

---

## Sequencing logic

1. **Clean and certify before you commercialise.** A pristine, safe, certified beach is the asset everything else monetises — so it comes first.
2. **Build the loops early.** Energy, water, and waste systems installed up front make every later project cheaper to run.
3. **Restore in parallel.** Dunes and mangroves take years to mature — start them in Phase 1.
4. **Commercialise once the foundation holds.** Resorts and watersports scale in Phase 2, on top of clean infrastructure and a willing community.
5. **Harden and replicate last.** Full resilience systems and the open digital twin in Phase 3 make the model durable and copyable.

## Critical dependencies

- Government approval and statutory constitution of the Authority (gateway to everything).
- CRZ clearances and EIA for shoreline works.
- Community consent and cooperative formation (livelihood-first).
- Catalytic capital for Phase 1; PPP appetite for Phase 2.
