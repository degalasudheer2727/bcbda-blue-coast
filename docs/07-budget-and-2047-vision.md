# Investment Plan, Growth Case & Vision 2047
### Bapatla–Chirala Beach Development Authority (BCBDA)

*Draft v0.3 — June 2026 · A ₹2,500 crore, three-phase programme aligned to India's Viksit Bharat @2047 mission*

---

## 1. The headline number

**₹2,500 crore across three phases** — a lean, affordable, public–private programme that front-loads a ₹1,500 cr Phase 1 to deliver a certifiable "proof beach," then adds two ₹500 cr phases to build out and harden the corridor.

| Phase | Years | Investment | Share | Goal |
|---|---|---:|---:|---|
| 1 — Foundations & proof beach | 1–3 | ₹1,500 cr | 60% | Certifiable Blue Flag beach + Authority established |
| 2 — Build-out | 4–6 | ₹500 cr | 20% | Year-round economy covering its own O&M |
| 3 — Maturity & Resilience | 7–10 | ₹500 cr | 20% | Replicable, fully self-sustaining model |
| **Total** | **10 yrs** | **₹2,500 cr** | **100%** | |

The structure keeps the public exchequer's risk to a minority share.

## 2. Why now — the growth case

This is **not a bet on demand appearing**; it is infrastructure meeting growth already underway:

- **Hyderabad's IT boom** is generating a vast, fast-growing weekend-and-leisure travel market that needs an accessible coast.
- **Amaravati**, the new state capital, is being developed roughly **60 km** from the corridor — a captive base of government, business, and residential demand on the doorstep.
- **NH-167A (Vodarevu–Piduguralla)** is being developed, plugging the coast directly into the interior and the capital region.
- **Rail connectivity to Bapatla** is improving rapidly, making day and overnight trips effortless statewide.
- A planned **coastal highway expansion** will string the corridor into a continuous, high-quality shoreline drive — the backbone of beach tourism.
- **Renowned local cuisine, a strong fishing economy, and improving civic infrastructure** give the corridor authentic character and a ready local supply base.

Together these mean a destination built now will open into rising, durable demand.

## 5. Affordability as a design rule

A core mandate: this is a **public beach for every family**, not an enclave.
- Parking, beach shacks, markets, and water games run on **capped, transparent pricing**.
- **Free and low-cost options** (public parks, free beach access, free two-wheeler parking bays) are reserved at every node.
- Local cooperatives and SHGs operate amenities, so value stays in the community and prices stay fair.

## 6. Expected returns (indicative)

- **₹2,500 cr** mobilised, ~44% from non-exchequer sources rising over time.
- **≥ 5,000** direct + indirect jobs (≥ 40% women).
- **~30 lakh** annual visitors at maturity, with the overnight share rising.
- **O&M self-sufficiency by ~Year 6**; operating surplus funding a conservation reserve thereafter.
- A **replicable template** for AP's 974+ km coast — the real prize.

## 7. Why 2047 (Viksit Bharat)

By 2047 — India's centenary — the ambition is a *Viksit Bharat*. The 2047 frame lets BCBDA think beyond the 10-year build to a destination trajectory, scaling the proven model along Bapatla's coast and franchising the "BCBDA template" to AP's wider shoreline.

**Out-of-the-box moves that justify the ambition:**
1. **A digital + VR twin of the coast** so authorities, investors, and citizens can *walk the future shoreline before a brick is laid* — de-risking decisions and accelerating consensus.
2. **Blue-carbon as an asset class** — monetise restored mangroves/dunes through verified credits.
3. **A "proof beach" before scale** — certify Suryalanka to Blue Flag first; let evidence unlock later capital.
4. **Community co-ownership equity** — turn potential opposition into aligned ownership.
5. **An energy-positive corridor** — export renewable surplus; the coast earns from sun and wind.

## 8. Civil-engineering & coastal-planning fundamentals

This plan is built on accepted coastal-engineering and urban-planning practice — and on India's own regulatory direction.

### 8.1 Soft engineering first (the national direction)
India is **shifting from hard structures (groynes, seawalls) to soft solutions** — beach nourishment and geosynthetics. In 2022 the National Green Tribunal directed states to prefer soft solutions, because **groynes trap sand up-drift and starve the down-drift coast**, simply moving erosion along the shore. BCBDA therefore leads with beach nourishment, dune + casuarina restoration, mangrove restoration, and geosynthetic/submerged structures only where engineering demands. Context: ~**33.6% of India's coastline is vulnerable to erosion**.

### 8.2 Setback & the building line
Respect the **CRZ High Tide Line (HTL) buffer**; keep permanent structures landward of the dune line; adopt a **climate-adjusted setback** for projected sea-level rise and surge to ~2050+, with all critical assets above that line.

### 8.3 Design for surge, flood & cyclone
**Elevated/stilted, cyclone-rated** construction; **integrated stormwater** (bioswales, retention wetlands, recharge wells) that cleans runoff before it reaches the sea; early-warning and shelter integration.

### 8.4 Sediment-cell & littoral thinking
Plan at the scale of the **littoral sediment cell**, not the single beach; model down-drift consequences before approval; monitor beach width and shoreline position for adaptive management (feeding the digital twin).

### 8.5 Carrying capacity & people-flow
Each node is designed to a **carrying capacity**; conservation reserves cap total intensity; **park-at-the-edge** keeps the active beach vehicle-free; **universal accessibility** per Blue Flag norms.
