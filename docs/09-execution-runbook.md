# Execution Runbook
### Bapatla–Chirala Beach Development Authority (BCBDA)

*Draft v0.1 — June 2026 · How the corridor goes from approval to a self-sustaining destination*

---

This runbook turns the plan into action. Each step names **what happens**, **who owns it**, and **what "done" looks like**.

## Step 0 — Mobilise (Months 0–6)
**Owner:** Government of AP + interim CEO
- Secure in-principle approval; constitute the Authority and Board; open the **BCBDA Fund**.
- Commission baseline surveys: coastal/topographic, ecology, hydrology, footfall; begin **EIA scoping**.
- Start community engagement via the Community Assembly.
- Publish the planning corpus and launch the website + GitHub repo.
- **Done when:** Authority is legally constituted and funded; baseline studies underway.

## Step 1 — Notify the statutory master plan
**Owner:** Authority + Town & Country Planning
- Notify the BCBDA area and zoning (Z0–Z5); stand up the **single-window** approval desk.
- Publish the plan, budget, and standards openly.
- **Done when:** the master plan is statutorily in force and approvals can be issued.

## Step 2 — Phase 1: Proof beach (₹1,500 cr)
**Owner:** Projects wing + contractors (with PPP partners for select assets)
- Deliver: **Suryalanka Blue Flag beach**, parking & mobility hubs, dune & casuarina restoration, the **waste + beach-cleaning** system, a **beachfront park**, first **shacks & waterfront market**, and the **micro-grid + EV-charging starter network**.
- Install lifeguarding, signage, ablutions, universal access, and water-quality testing.
- **Done when:** Suryalanka meets Blue Flag standards and is open, clean, safe, and busy.

## Step 3 — Stand up O&M & cooperatives
**Owner:** Operations + Community wings
- Form and train local cooperatives: cleaning **"Blue Brigade,"** shacks, watersports, tours, parking.
- Put maintenance contracts and **SLAs** in place; begin **fee/revenue collection**; distribute the first **community benefit share**.
- **Done when:** the beach is maintained to standard by funded, trained local teams.

## Step 4 — Phase 2: Build-out (₹500 cr)
**Owner:** Authority + PPP partners
- Extend to **Vodarevu watersports**, the **estuary mangrove reserve**, **eco-resort & wellness concessions**, the **green-blue spine** (NMT + e-shuttle), and the **skilling campus**.
- Scale EV charging, water reuse, and waste loops corridor-wide.
- **Done when:** O&M is self-funded from own revenue (~Year 6) and the corridor draws year-round.

## Step 5 — Phase 3: Maturity & resilience (₹500 cr)
**Owner:** Authority + research partners
- Deliver the **zero-discharge water system**, **Ramapuram conservation & research station**, full **coastal resilience & early-warning**, and the **open digital twin / VR platform**.
- Pursue a **second Blue Flag** certification.
- **Done when:** all critical assets are resilient, water discharge is zero-untreated, and the twin is live.

## Step 6 — Monitor, audit & replicate
**Owner:** Authority + independent auditors
- Track all KPIs on the **public dashboard**; run annual **financial + environmental/social audits**.
- Publish the **replication playbook** for the wider AP coast.
- **Done when:** the model is proven, audited, and being copied elsewhere.

---

## RACI at a glance

| Function | Leads |
|---|---|
| Approvals, statute, single-window | Authority (Planning) + Govt of AP |
| Coastal works, beach, dunes | Projects wing + coastal-engineering contractors |
| Energy, EV, water, waste | Operations + PPP operators |
| Shacks, markets, watersports, cleaning | Community wing + local cooperatives |
| Finance, fund, audit | Finance wing + independent auditors |
| Ecology, monitoring, resilience | Environment & Resilience wing + research partners |

## Critical dependencies
Government approval and statute (gateway to all) · CRZ/EIA clearances · community consent & cooperative formation · catalytic Phase-1 capital · PPP appetite for Phase 2.
